Steak Recipe

Steak is humanity’s primal flex—just a hunk of cow, cooked with enough precision that people will argue for hours about whether medium-rare is perfection or if well-done should be classified as a crime.

At its core, steak is a cut of beef (ribeye, sirloin, filet mignon, T-bone, etc.), seasoned simply—usually just salt and pepper—then cooked hot and fast to lock in juices and create that golden-brown crust called the Maillard reaction (basically flavor alchemy).

The beauty of steak is in its minimalism: meat, fire, and timing. Done right, it’s tender, juicy, and full of flavor. Done wrong, it’s shoe leather you could resole a boot with.

Culturally, it’s loaded with symbolism—wealth, masculinity, indulgence, “treat yourself” energy. It can show up in a Michelin-star plate with red wine reduction or on a cheap backyard grill with nothing but a splash of beer to keep it moist.

Ingredients (classic pan-seared steak):

  • 1 good cut of beef (ribeye, sirloin, filet mignon, T-bone—whatever you can afford)
  • Salt (coarse or flaky, not table dust)
  • Black pepper (freshly cracked, otherwise don’t bother)
  • Butter (a few knobs)
  • Garlic cloves (smashed, skin on is fine)
  • Fresh herbs (thyme, rosemary, or both)
  • Olive oil (for the pan)

Steps:

  1. Prep the steak.

    2. – Take it out of the fridge 30–60 minutes before cooking (cold steak = uneven cook).

    – Pat it dry with paper towels.

    – Season aggressively with salt and pepper on both sides.

  2. Heat the pan.

    3. – Use a heavy pan (cast iron if you’ve got it).

    – Heat it until it’s almost smoking, then add a splash of olive oil.

  3. Sear.

    4. – Lay the steak in the pan and leave it alone for 2–3 minutes. Don’t poke, don’t move, don’t fidget.

    – Flip and sear the other side the same way.

  4. Butter baste.

    5. – Lower the heat slightly.

    – Add butter, garlic, and herbs to the pan.

    – Tilt the pan and spoon the melted butter over the steak repeatedly for flavor and moisture.

  5. Cook to temp.

    6. – Rare: 2–3 min per side

    – Medium-rare: 3–4 min per side

    – Medium: 4–5 min per side

    – Anything beyond that, you’re committing culinary violence, but technically it’s 6+ min per side.

  6. Rest.

    7. – Remove the steak from the pan and let it sit for 5–10 minutes before cutting. Juices need to redistribute or you’ll end up with beef soup on the cutting board.

  7. Serve.

    8. – Slice against the grain if it’s a big cut

    – Optional sprinkle of flaky salt.

    – That’s it—no sauce needed unless you messed up.
