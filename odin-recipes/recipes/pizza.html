Pizza Recipe

Pizza is basically edible real estate: a flat piece of dough with a property development of sauce, cheese, and toppings. Born in Naples as street food for workers, it somehow mutated into a global obsession where every culture slaps its own personality on top—Americans drown it in cheese, Italians argue about simplicity, and the rest of the world experiments like it’s a science fair project.

At its best, pizza is three things: a crisp yet chewy crust, tangy tomato sauce, and molten cheese that strings halfway across the room when you bite. From Margherita (tomato, mozzarella, basil) to monstrosities like barbecue chicken stuffed-crust, pizza’s versatility is its weapon. It can be a quick snack, a guilty pleasure, or an artisanal masterpiece baked in a 500°C wood-fired oven.

It’s communal, it’s messy, and it’s scientifically engineered to make you forget your diet.

Ingredients (basic Margherita level):

  • Dough (flour, water, yeast, salt, olive oil, maybe sugar if you’re cheating)
  • Tomato sauce (canned tomatoes, olive oil, garlic, oregano, salt)
  • Cheese (mozzarella is the classic, but people dump everything from cheddar to pineapple on there)
  • Toppings (basil leaves if you want to feel Italian, pepperoni if you want to feel American, whatever’s in your fridge if you’re a student)

Steps:

  1. Make the dough (or buy it, I won’t judge).

    2. – Mix flour, water, yeast, salt, olive oil. Knead until smooth.

    – Let it rise for 1–2 hours until it doubles in size.

  2. Prep the sauce.

    3. – Heat olive oil, add garlic, dump in canned tomatoes, season with salt, oregano, maybe basil. Simmer until thick.

  3. Shape the base.

    4. – Flatten the dough into a circle (or square if you gave up). Don’t roll too thin unless you want cracker vibes.

  4. Assemble.

    5. – Spread sauce.

    – Add cheese.

    – Add toppings, but don’t bury it like a casserole—pizza is not lasagna.

  5. Bake.

    6. – Oven at max heat (250°C / 480°F if yours doesn’t suck).

    – Bake 7–12 minutes until the crust is browned and cheese bubbling.

  6. Finish.

    7. – Fresh basil, olive oil drizzle, done.

    – Eat recklessly, burn the roof of your mouth, complain, then keep eating.
